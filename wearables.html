Research

Wearables

To state the obvious just about every device we require is already available. There is a growing range of fitness/activity trackers and health device for monitoring and tracking fitness-related metrics such as distance walked or run, calorie consumption, heartrate, quality of sleep, blood-glucose, and body functions etc.

These wearable devices are synced, in many cases wirelessly, to a computer or smartphone for long-term data tracking. There are also independent smartphone and Facebook apps.

Our Conclusion

We decided to use Fitbit Charge HR as it measures the heart rate as well as saving data regarding sleep, nutrition and activity. Furthermore, it can be synchronised with GoogleFit which is what we need.
